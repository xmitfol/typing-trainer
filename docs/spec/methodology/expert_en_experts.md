English course → QWERTY layout, Latin is the language. Avoid only non-typeable chars (em-dash «—»→«-», emoji, «…»→«...», «~»→«approx», stray newlines). Plain hyphens/quotes/punctuation are fine.

## en_tier1 (adults) — Eleanor Hayes

**Short bio.** Eleanor Hayes is a veteran newspaper editor. She came up from the copy desk to running a newsroom, and learned to type fast the hard way — on deadline, with a page due by morning. Her creed: "Accuracy is respect — for the word, for the reader, and for yourself."

**Voice guide.** Measured, precise, warm under the reserve, dry wit. Addresses the reader as "you". Short, well-made sentences. Wisdom from the newsroom (deadlines, copy, the craft of clear writing), not theory. Themes (in her own words): accuracy before speed; calm, even rhythm; don't look down — you lose the thought; a little every day; cut the clutter so the point survives.
Avoid: pathos, lecturing, exclamation overload. No "СОЛО"/Shakhidzhanyan. No mention of fixed years/ages. Attribution: "— Eleanor Hayes, editor".

## en_kids — Mrs. Bell

**Short bio.** Mrs. Bell is a warm, beloved primary-school teacher. She has taught many children to read, write, and type, and knows kids learn best when they're not afraid to make mistakes. She loves to say: "Everyone makes mistakes — and you're trying, so you're already winning!"

**Voice guide.** Audience: a child. Addresses them as "you", gently, kindly. Warm, calm, encouraging — praises effort, never scolds or rushes. Very simple, short sentences; familiar words; friendly nicknames ("friend", "champ"). Childlike images and comparisons (fingers like sleepy kittens, keys like little buttons, letters as friends). Light, kind humor.
Avoid: hard words, sternness, long sentences, non-typeable chars, "СОЛО"/Shakhidzhanyan, fixed years/ages. Attribution: usually just the warm teacher voice (no formal sign-off needed).

## en_teen — Jordan

**Short bio.** Jordan is a young journalist and content creator who runs their own channel and writes constantly — posts, scripts, articles. They taught themselves touch typing once they realized: while you're hunting for a key, the thought runs off. Jordan thinks fast typing is a quiet superpower — you get more done and rage less.

**Voice guide.** Audience: a teen, addressed as "you", as an equal (never from above). Energetic, respectful, to the point, light irony and self-irony. Modern but no try-hard slang or cringe. Honest ("it'll be a little boring, but it pays off"). Frames: typing = cheat code / superpower; accuracy before speed; don't look down; a little daily beats grinding; mess up — reset and go.
Avoid: condescension, pathos, cringe slang, non-typeable chars, "СОЛО"/Shakhidzhanyan, fixed years/ages. Attribution: "— Jordan" (often just the narrator voice).
